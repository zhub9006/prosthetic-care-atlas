# Regional Coverage Gap Analysis

## 1. Rural West Virginia

### Geographic Context
- **Center**: 38.48°N, 80.84°W
- **Bounding box**: 37.20°N–40.64°N, 82.64°W–77.72°W
- **Population**: ~1.77 million | 97% rural

### Provider Landscape
- Very few prosthetics providers; concentrated in Charleston, Huntington, Morgantown
- 4 VA medical centers (Morgantown, Charleston, Huntington, Martinsburg)
- Most residents travel 2+ hours for fittings

### Key Barriers
| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Mountainous terrain | Road travel 2-3x longer than distance |
| Provider shortage | Few CP/CO per capita |
| Insurance gaps | High uninsured rates in Appalachian counties |
| Weather | Seasonal road closures isolate communities |

### Gaps
- **CRITICAL**: No prosthetics providers south of Beckley except via VA referral
- **HIGH**: No pediatric prosthetic specialists in the state
- **MODERATE**: Limited osseointegration and myoelectric technology

## 2. Eastern Kentucky (Appalachian Region)

### Geographic Context
- **Center**: 37.25°N, 83.20°W (near Hazard, Perry County)
- **Bounding box**: 36.9°N–37.5°N, 82.8°W–84.5°W
- **Population**: ~500,000 in Appalachian foothills | 94% rural

### Provider Landscape
- **Zero** prosthetics providers in core Appalachian counties (Perry, Knott, Letcher, Clay, Harlan)
- No orthotic fitting facilities; all care requires 3+ hour travel
- VA closest in Lexington or Augusta, GA

### Key Barriers
| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Isolation | Single-lane mountain roads |
| Economic distress | Lowest per capita income in the US |
| Opioid crisis legacy | High amputee population; few local options |
| Specialist scarcity | All care is referral-based |

### Gaps
- **CRITICAL**: Zero prosthetics providers in core counties
- **CRITICAL**: No orthotic fitting facilities
- **HIGH**: No prosthetic rehabilitation programs

## 3. Mississippi Delta

### Geographic Context
- **Center**: ~34.00°N, 90.20°W
- **Bounding box**: 33.5°N–35.0°N, 88.5°W–91.0°W
- **Population**: ~1 million across MS/AR/LA | 85% rural

### Provider Landscape
- No prosthetics providers within the Delta proper
- Nearest: Memphis, TN (~40 miles from MS Delta)
- VA Memphis and VA Jackson are the closest
- Pediatric services only through Memphis-based providers

### Key Barriers
| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Poverty | Highest poverty rates in the US |
| Transportation | 50-100+ mile distances, limited public transit |
| Insurance | High Medicaid dependency; variable prosthetic coverage |
| Provider density | <0.5 prosthetists per 100K in core Delta counties |

### Gaps
- **CRITICAL**: No prosthetics providers in MS Delta counties (Bolivar, Coahoma, Humphreys, Sunflower, Washington, Sharkey, Issaquena, Leflore, Tallahatchie)
- **CRITICAL**: No orthotic providers in the Delta
- **HIGH**: Pediatric prosthetic access nearly nonexistent
